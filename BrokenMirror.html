The Broken Mirror Superstition

Exploring the myths and beliefs surrounding broken mirrors.

What is the Broken Mirror Superstition?

Many cultures believe that breaking a mirror brings seven years of bad luck. This superstition has roots in ancient beliefs about mirrors reflecting not just physical appearances but also the soul.

Historical Background

The origins of this superstition can be traced back to the Romans, who believed that mirrors had mystical properties. They thought that breaking a mirror would damage one's soul, leading to misfortune.The superstition surrounding broken mirrors dates back to ancient Rome, where it was believed that mirrors held a reflection of the soul. Breaking a mirror was thought to bring seven years of bad luck, a belief that emerged in 15th century Italy when glass mirrors became more common. This idea likely stemmed from the ancient Romans' understanding of life cycles and renewal.

How to Avoid Bad Luck

If you accidentally break a mirror, some suggest throwing the pieces into murky water or burying them under a full moon to ward off bad luck.

Cultural References:

In various cultures, breaking a mirror is often dramatized in movies or literature, where characters face dire consequences after the incident. For instance, a character in a horror film might break a mirror and subsequently encounter supernatural events, reinforcing the belief in the curse.
